How it works. Interest each month is charged on the outstanding balance (monthly rest), the way Malaysian banks calculate it. MRTA is a one-time premium — when financed into the loan it accrues interest; otherwise it's an upfront out-of-pocket cost. MLTA is a recurring monthly premium counted separately, since it doesn't reduce your principal.

Most buyers take MRTA or MLTA, not both — leave the one you don't use at 0. MLTA total assumes the premium stops when the loan clears; real policies may run a fixed term. Figures exclude legal fees, stamp duty and early-settlement penalties. Planning tool only — not financial advice.
